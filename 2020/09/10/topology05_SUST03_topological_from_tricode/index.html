What is topological order - a view from topological excitations

Goal of this lecture use toric-code models to demonstrate the concept of (Abelian) topological excitations - a key concept in topological orders \(-\) and show some of their properties.

What is topological order?

Definition: A \(d\) -dimensional gapped quantum many-body system carries topological order, if the ground state(s) is/are long-range entangled.

A ground state is long-range entangled if and only if it cannot be adiabatically connected to the trivial product state by finite-depth local unitary transformations without closing the gap.

如果我们能找到一个 local unitary transformations把系统变成 trivial product state，我们可以证明它是一个 trivial的态。如果它不是 trivial的，我们不可能对每个态都做trivial product state，比较好的方式是寻找“拓扑不变量”

Manifestation of topological order:

  • topological excitations
  • non-vanishing topological entanglement entropy,
  • chiral edge states,
  • fractional charges, etc.

Topological excitations

Topological excitations may be particle-like, line-like, etc. They cannot be created/destroyed by any operators supported around the excitations themselves.

例如二维的“任意子”

Intrinsically topological properties:

  • fusion rules \(a \times b \rightarrow \sum_{c} N_{a b}^{c} c\)
  • quantum dimension \(d_{a}\)
  • topological spin \(\theta_{a}\)
  • a fusion matrix \(F_{a b c},\) braiding matrix \(R_{a b},\) etc

不仅需要拓扑，还需要对称性，才有的性质

Additional properties (e.g., in presence of symmetry):

  • a fractional charge 例如分数霍尔效应
  • a quantized Hall conductance, etc

Topological excitations in \(2 D\)

Toric-code model (Kitaev '97)

名命code，来源于量子计算，stable code，可以严格解的多体模型，而且内容丰富。

  1. Hilbert space

Spin \(1 / 2\) on each link of an \(L \times L\) lattice: \[ V =\otimes_{l} V _{l}, \quad V _{l}=\operatorname{span}\{|\uparrow\rangle,|\downarrow\rangle\} \] Total dimension \(\operatorname{dim}( V )=2^{2 L^{2}}\)

  1. Hamiltonian

\[ H=-\sum_{v} A_{v}-\sum_{p} B_{p} \] where

  • \(A_{v}=\prod_{i \in \text { neigh }(v)} \sigma_{i}^{x},\)
  • $ B_{p}={i (p)} {i}^{z}$

Hamiltonian一般是local的，附近相互作用。求解意味者对角化，对角化先看对称性。很多的对称性 \(2 L^{2}\)，所以可解。

One can check:

  • \(\left[A_{v}, A_{v^{\prime}}\right]=\left[B_{p}, B_{p^{\prime}}\right]=\left[A_{v}, B_{p}\right]=0\) \(\Rightarrow\left[A_{v}, H\right]=\left[B_{p}, H\right]=0\)

  • \(A_{v}^{2}=B_{p}^{2}=1\)

Accordingly, eigenstates of \(H\) are \[ \begin{array}{l} A_{v}\left|\left\{a_{v}\right\},\left\{b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle=a_{v}\left|\left\{a_{v}\right\},\left\{b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle \\ B_{p}\left|\left\{a_{v}\right\},\left\{b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle=b_{p}\left|\left\{a_{v}\right\},\left\{b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle \end{array} \] where \(a_{v}, b_{p}=\pm 1\) and \(\mu\) labels degenerate states. The energy is \(E=-\sum_{v} a_{v}-\sum_{p} b_{p}\) The ground states are \(\left|a_{v}=b_{p}=1, \mu\right\rangle,\) with \(E_{0}=-2 L^{2}\)

What is \(\mu ?\) Note that

  • \(\#\) of \(v^{\prime} s=\#\) of \(p^{\prime} s=L^{2}\) \(\Rightarrow\) possible choices of \(\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}=2^{2 L^{2}}\)
  • \(\operatorname{dim}( V )=2^{2 L^{2}}\)

However, two constraints \[ \prod_{v} A_{v}=1, \quad \prod_{p} B_{p}=1 \] i.e., \(\prod_{v} a_{v}=\prod_{p} b_{p}=1 .\) So, only \(\left(L^{2}-1\right)\) independent \(a_{v}\) and \(b_{p},\) respectively. Claim: for fixed \(\left\{a_{v}\right\}\) and \(\left\{b_{p}\right\},\) each state is 4 -fold degenerate.

能谱，高简并度。4从简并有丰富的内容。4从简并也是一种对称性。

Degeneracy

  • \(S_{1}\) 是红线，边缘线，可以转角
  • \(S_{2}\) 是蓝线，方格中间的点连起来

Define \[ S_{1}=\prod_{i \in l_{1}} \sigma_{i}^{z}, \quad S_{2}=\prod_{i \in l_{2}} \sigma_{i}^{\hat{x}} \] since \[ \begin{aligned} \left[S_{i}, A_{v}\right] &=\left[S_{i}, B_{p}\right]=0, \quad\left[S_{i}, H\right]=0 \\ \left[S_{1}, S_{2}\right] &=0, \quad S_{i}^{2}=1 \end{aligned} \] So, we have \[ \begin{array}{l} S_{1}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu_{1}, \mu_{2}\right\rangle=\mu_{1}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu_{1}, \mu_{2}\right\rangle \\ S_{2}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu_{1}, \mu_{2}\right\rangle=\mu_{2}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu_{1}, \mu_{2}\right\rangle \end{array} \] where \(\mu_{1}, \mu_{2}=\pm 1\)

\(S_{1}\) 也是对称性

Two remarks:

  1. We may also choose the lines \(l_{1}\) and \(l_{2}\) along \(y\) direction, for the operators \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\)

  2. Operators \(A_{v}, B_{p}, S_{1}, S_{2}\) can be thought of as symmetries of \(H,\) as they commute with \(H\) However,

  • $ A_{v}$ and \(B_{p}\) act on spins around a "point" 点状的对称性
  • $ S_{1}$ and \(S_{2}\) act on spins along a "line" 线状的对称性

This is in contrast to the usual global symmetries, which act on all spins in the system (a "surface" ).

延展

Toric-code模型不仅可以定义在 GSD=4 有4个边，也可以是两个洞的。希尔伯特空间的自由度减去对称性的自由度，得到欧拉数

Toric-code model can be defined on any lattice and high-genus surface, with \[ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{dim}( V ) &=2^{E} \\ \text { choices of }\left\{a_{v}\right\} &=2^{V} \\ \text { choices of }\left\{b_{p}\right\} &=2^{F} \end{aligned} \] and two constraints \(\prod A_{v}=\prod B_{p}=1\). Then, \[ GSD =2^{E-(V-1)-(F-1)}=2^{2-2(1-g)} \] \[ =4^{g} \] where \(2(1-g)=V-E+F\) is the Euler characteristic.

Excited states

Excitations are associated with \(a_{v}=-1\) for some vertices and / or \(b_{p}=-1\) for some plaquettes.

基态是怎么到激发态的

There are four types of point-like excitations: \[ 1, e, m, \epsilon \] where "1" is the vacuum. They can be created from ground states by applying string operators: \[ S_{e}=\prod_{i \in l_{1}} \sigma_{i}^{z}, \quad S_{m}=\prod_{i \in l_{2}} \sigma_{i}^{x} \] Closed string operators do not create excitations.

Under fusion of excitations, we have \[ \begin{aligned} e \times e &=1 \\ m \times m &=1 \\ \epsilon \times \epsilon &=1 \\ e \times m &=\epsilon \\ e \times \epsilon &=m \\ m \times \epsilon &=e \\ 1 \times x &=x, \forall x \end{aligned} \] Note that fusion is symmetric.

Prove

Prove \(e, m, \epsilon\) are topological excitations

Recall the definition of topological excitation: An excitation is topological if and only if it cannot be created/annilihilated by local operators.

Claim: Anyons \(e, m, \epsilon\) are topological excitations.

反证法

Proof: Consider a state that contains an \(e\) (as well as other anyons) and suppose that \(e\) can be annihilated by some local operator \(\hat{O}\). Then,

since \(\hat{O}\) is supported around \(e,\) we have \(S_{m} \hat{O}=\hat{O} S_{m} .\) Then

It is a contradiction. So, \(e\) is a topological excitation, and similarly for \(m\) and \(\epsilon .\)

General theory of anyons

Algebraic theory of Abelian anyons contains

  1. A set of topological excitations (anyons): \(C =\{1, a, b, \ldots\}\)
  2. A set of fusion rules: \(a \times b=c .\) In particular, if \(c=1, b \equiv \bar{a}\) is the anti-particle of \(a\)
  3. The mutual statistics \(\theta_{a, b}\) and the exchange statistics \(\theta_{a}\)

Algebraic theory of non-Abelian anyons contains

  1. A set of topological excitations (anyons): \(C =\{1, a, b, \ldots\}\)
  2. A set of fusion rules: \(a \times b=\sum_{c} N_{a b}^{c} c .\) In particular, if \(c=1, b \equiv \bar{a}\) is the anti-particle of \(a\)
  3. Additional fusion properties (described by matrices \(F_{a b c}\) ) and braiding properties \(\left(R_{a, b}, S, T,\right.\) etc \()\)

All these combined is mathematically named Category theory(I'm not being very rigorous here). For references, see e.g., Kitaev \(2006,\) Zhang & Kong's notes.

Toric-code model with open boundary

Hamiltonian \[ H=-\sum_{v \in \text { bulk }} A_{v}-\sum_{p} B_{p}-\sum_{v \in \text { edge }} \tilde{A}_{v}-\sum_{v \in \text { corner }} \tilde{\tilde{A}}_{v} \] where \[ \begin{aligned} A_{v}, \tilde{A}_{v}, \tilde{\tilde{A}}_{v}=& \prod_{i \in \operatorname{neigh}(v)} \sigma_{i}^{x} \\ B_{p}=& \prod_{i \in \operatorname{edge}(p)} \sigma_{i}^{z} \end{aligned} \] It can be solved as before, as all \(A\) 's and \(B\) 's commute. Eigenstates can be labelled completely by quantum numbers associated with \(A\) 's and \(B\) 's, with no extra degeneracy.

也是可以严格可解

Topological excitations on the boundary

Dimensionality matters for identifying topological excitations.

On the edge, \(m\) particle can be created or annihilated, allowing a single \(m\) to exist in the bulk. Accordingly, the set of topological excitations on the edge contains \[ C _{\text {edge }}=\{1, e\} \] with \(1 \sim m\) and \(e \sim \epsilon .\) More generally, anyon condensation theory describes gapped boundary. Comment: there is another "rough" boundary condition in which \(e\) condenses, such that \(C _{\text {edge }}=\{1, m\}\)

  • 把粒子挪到边界

  • \(\tilde{A}_{v} S_{e}|\psi\rangle=-S_{e}\tilde{A}_{v} |\psi\rangle=- S_{e}|\psi\rangle\)

  • \(B_{\rho} S_{m}|\psi\rangle= S_{m}B_{\rho} |\psi\rangle=1 S_{m}|\psi\rangle\)

  • 在边界的时候，\(B_{p}\) 会被local operator \(\sigma^{x}\) 弄消失，所以这时候它不是 拓扑激发。

Topological excitations in \(3 D\)

The Hamiltonian is \[ H=-\sum_{v} A_{v}-\sum_{p} B_{p} \] where

  • \(A_{v}=\sigma_{1}^{x} \sigma_{2}^{x} \sigma_{3}^{x} \sigma_{4}^{x} \sigma_{5}^{x} \sigma_{6}^{x}\)

  • \(B_{p}=\sigma_{1}^{z} \sigma_{2}^{z} \sigma_{3}^{z} \sigma_{4}^{z}\)

One can check that all \(A_{v}\) and \(B_{p}\) commute.

Diagonalize \(H\)

Eigenstates of \(H\) can be labelled as \(\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle,\) with \[ A_{v}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle=a_{v}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle, \quad B_{p}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle=b_{p}\left|\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\}, \mu\right\rangle \] and \(E=-\sum_{v} a_{v}-\sum_{p} b_{p} .\) The ground state energy is \(E_{0}=-4 L^{3}\)

其中 \(A_{v}\) 是 \(L^3\), \(B_{p}\) 是 \(3L^3\)

Many constraints for periodic boundary condition: \[ \begin{align}\prod_{v} A_{v}=1 ; \quad \prod_{p} B_{p}=1 ; \\ \prod_{p \in Q } B_{p}=1 \forall Q \\ \prod_{p \in \text { xy plane }} B_{p}=\prod_{p \in \text { yz plane }} B_{p}=\prod_{p \in \text { zx plane }} B_{p}=1\end{align} \] giving rise to \(\left(L^{3}-1\right)\) independent \(A_{v}\) and \(\left(2 L^{3}-2\right)\) independent \(B_{p} .\) Accordingly, for fixed \(\left\{a_{v}, b_{p}\right\},\) the degeneracy is 8

其中\(Q\)表示立方体unit cell，数量 \(L^3\)

The degeneracy corresponds to the following line-like or membrane-like symmetries: \[ S_{x}=\prod_{i \in-} \sigma_{i}^{z}, \quad S_{y}=\prod_{i \in I } \sigma_{i}^{z}, \quad M_{x y}=\prod_{i \in I } \sigma_{i}^{x} \] One can check that they mutually commute, and commute with all \(A_{v}\) and \(B_{p} .\) The 8 -fold degeneracy are labelled by the eigenvalues ±1 of \(S_{x}, S_{y}\) and \(M_{x y}\). These line-like and membrane operators can be chosen along other directions, i.e., \(S_{z}, M_{z x}\) and \(M_{y z}\)

Excitations are associated with \(a_{v}=-1\) for some vertices and / or \(b_{p}=-1\) for some plaquettes.

There is one kind of particle-like topological excitation, \(e,\) and two kinds of loop-like topological excitations, \(m\) and \(\epsilon \equiv m \times e .\) Further more, loop excitations may be knotted.

纽结

Braiding statistics between particles and loops

Particle-like excitations and loop-like excitations are created by string operators and membrane operators, respectively.

If \(e\) winds around an \(m\) loop, one obtains a \(\pi\) braiding statistical phase, similarly to \(2 D\). With this non-trivial braiding, one can show that \(e\) and \(m\) are indeed topological excitations, similarly as in \(2 D\).

Braiding statistics between loops

One may also define braiding statistics between loop excitations.

Two-loop braiding can always be reduced to particle-loop braiding statistics.

Three-loop braiding carries more topological information than particle-loop braiding (unfortunately, not in toric code).

For \(3 D\) toric code, the mutual loop braiding statistics is \(\theta_{m, m}=\theta_{\epsilon, \epsilon}=0\) and \(\theta_{m, \epsilon}=\pi\)

Topological excitations and their fusion and braiding properties characterize much of the underlying topological order in quantum many-body systems.
