Boltzmann equation

Distribution function: \(f ( r , k , t )\) \[ f(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t) d^{3} r \cdot 2 \frac{d^{3} k}{(2 \pi)^{3}}=\text{Number of electrons within $d ^{3} r$ and $d ^{3} k$ around $( r , k )$ at time $t$} \] example \[ \vec{j}(\vec{r}, t)=-e \int 2 \frac{d^{3} k}{(2 \pi)^{3}} \dot{\vec{r}} f(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t) \]

分布函数，对路程一阶导的积分，就是电流。

Evolution of the distribution function

分布函数 的时间演化

  1. 没碰撞
  2. 有碰撞
  • \(t \rightarrow t+d t\)

\[ \begin{aligned} f(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t) \rightarrow & f(\vec{r}+d \vec{r}, \vec{k}+d \vec{k}, t+d t) \\ &=f(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t)+\frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{r}} \cdot d \vec{r}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{k}} \cdot \vec{k}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} d t \end{aligned} \]

1 without collision

the phase-space density does not change in the comoving frame \[ \frac{d f}{d t}=\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}+\dot{\vec{r}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{r}}+\dot{\vec{k}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{k}}=0 \] Phase space is incompressible (Liouville's Theorem)

2 With collision

collision due to disorder...

Boltzmann eq. \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}+\dot{\vec{r}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{r}}+\dot{\vec{k}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{k}}=\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\right)_{c o l l} \] where

  • \(\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\right)_{c o l l}\) comes from Source/drain

Transition rate

Transition rate for an electron at \(k \rightarrow k ^{\prime}:\) \[ W _{ k , k } \text { ( calculated by Fermi golden rule) } \] In a crowded space, one needs to consider occupancy and summation \[ W_{k, k^{\prime}} \rightarrow \sum_{k^{\prime}} f_{k}\left(1-f_{k^{\prime}}\right) W_{k, k^{\prime}} \] On the other hand, the transition rate for an electron to be scattered into \(k\) \[ \sum_{k^{\prime}}\left(1-f_{k}\right) f_{k} W_{k^{\prime}, k} \] therefore, the Source term \[ \begin{aligned} \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\right)_{c o l l} &=\sum_{k^{\prime}}\left[\left(1-f_{k}\right) f_{k} W_{k^{\prime}, k}-f_{k}\left(1-f_{k^{\prime}}\right) W_{k, k^{\prime}}\right] \\ &=\sum_{k^{\prime}}\left(f_{k^{\prime}}-f_{k}\right) W_{k^{\prime}, k} \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(W_{k, k^{\prime}}=W_{k^{\prime} ; k}\) for elastic scattering

for uniform \(E, B, T\) fields \(f_{k}=f_{k}^{0}+\vec{C} \cdot \vec{k}\) then \[ \begin{aligned} \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\right)_{c o l l} &=-\vec{C} \cdot \sum_{k^{\prime}}\left(\vec{k}-\vec{k}^{\prime}\right) W_{k^{\prime}, k} \\ &=-\vec{C} \cdot \vec{k} \sum_{k^{\prime}}\left(1-\hat{k} \cdot \hat{k}^{\prime}\right) W_{k^{\prime}, k} \\ &=-\frac{f_{k}-f_{k}^{0}}{\tau_{\varepsilon}} \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(\frac{1}{\tau_{\varepsilon}}=\sum_{k^{\prime}}\left(1-\hat{k} \cdot \hat{k}^{\prime}\right) W_{k^{\prime}, k}\) is Transport relaxation time

if the amplitude \(\left(k=k^{\prime}\right)\), Only the component of \(k\) // \(k\) contribute to the integral (if \(W\) depends only on \(\theta\) )

Note: for inelastic scatterings, detailed balance requires \[ \begin{aligned} \left(1-f_{k}^{0}\right) f_{k^{\prime}}^{0} W_{k^{\prime}, k}&=f_{k}^{0}\left(1-f_{k^{\prime}}^{0}\right) W_{k, k^{\prime}} \\ e^{\frac{\varepsilon(\vec{k})-\mu}{k_{B} T}} W_{k^{\prime}, k}&=e^{\frac{\varepsilon\left(\vec{k}^{\prime}\right)-\mu}{k_{B} T}} W_{k, k^{\prime}} \end{aligned} \] where we use \(f^{0}(\vec{k})=\frac{1}{e^{[\varepsilon(\vec{k})-\mu] / k_{B} T}+1}\)

Relaxation time approximation

\[ \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\right)_{c o l l}=-\frac{f(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t)-f_{0}(\vec{r}, \vec{k})}{\tau(\varepsilon(\vec{k}))} \]

where

  • Unperturbed equilibrium dist \(f_{0}(\vec{r}, \vec{k})=\frac{1}{\exp \left(\frac{\varepsilon(\vec{k})-\mu(\vec{r})}{k_{B} T(\vec{r})}\right)+1}\) Density and temperature gradients are allowed
  • \(\tau(\varepsilon(\vec{k}))\) Relaxation (allows energy dependence)

Boltzmann equation

Boltzmann equation \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}+\dot{\vec{r}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{r}}+\dot{\vec{k}} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \vec{k}}=\frac{d f}{d t}=-\frac{f-f^{0}}{\tau_{\varepsilon}} \]

其中

  • \(\frac{d f_{0}}{d t}=\dot{\vec{r}} \cdot \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \vec{r}}+\dot{\vec{k}} \cdot \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \vec{k}}\)
  • \(\begin{aligned} \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \vec{r}} &=\left.\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \mu}\right|_{T} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial T}\right|_{\mu} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}} \\ &=\left[-\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}-\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}\right] \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon} \end{aligned}\)
  • \(\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \vec{k}}=\hbar \dot{\vec{r}} \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\)

Chamber's formulation

\[ \begin{aligned} f_{r k}(t) &=\frac{1}{\tau} \int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} f_{r k}^{0}\left(t^{\prime}\right) e^{-(t-t) / \tau} \\ \text { or } &=f_{r k}^{0}-\int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} e^{-\left(t-t^{\prime}\right) / \tau} \frac{d f_{r k}^{0}\left(t^{\prime}\right)}{d t^{\prime}} \end{aligned} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} f_{r k}(t) &=f_{r k}^{0}+\int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} e^{-(t-t) / \tau} \vec{v}_{k} \cdot\left[\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}+\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}-\hbar \dot{\vec{k}}\right] \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon} \\ &=f_{0}+\delta f \end{aligned} \]

Let's call this “Chamber's formulation"

example

温差和电场，所以系统会产生电流和热流。并且两者会相互影响，所以可以写成矩阵关系。

consider a system with electric field and temperature gradient, but no magnetic field

Electric current density \[ \begin{aligned} \vec{j}(\vec{r}, t) &=-e \int[d k] \dot{\vec{r}}(\vec{r}, \vec{k}, t)\\ &=-e \int\left[d k \right]\vec{v}_{k} f_{0}-e \int\left[d k\right] \vec{v}_{k} \delta f \end{aligned} \] where

  • \([d k] \equiv 2 \frac{d^{3} k}{(2 \pi)^{3}}\)
  • \(\dot{\vec{r}}=\frac{1}{\hbar} \frac{\partial \varepsilon}{\partial \vec{k}}\)
  • \(\hbar \dot{\vec{k}}=-e \vec{E}\)

For steady perturbations \[ \begin{aligned} \delta f &=\left(\int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} e^{-\left(t-t^{\prime}\right) / \tau}\right) \vec{v} \cdot\left[e \vec{E}+\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}+\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}\right] \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon} \\ &=\tau \vec{v} \cdot\left[e \vec{G}+\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}\right] \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon} \end{aligned} \] where \(\vec{G} \equiv \vec{E}+\frac{1}{e} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}\) Electrochemical "force"

Electric current density become \[ \begin{aligned} &\vec{j}(\vec{r}, t)=e \int[d k] \tau \vec{v} \vec{v} \cdot\left[e \vec{G}+\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}\right]\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right)\\ &= L _{11} \vec{G}+ L _{12}\left(-\frac{\nabla T}{T}\right) \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(L _{11}\) is \(\sigma\)
  • \(\sigma\) is conductivity tensor

Thermal current density \[ \begin{aligned} \vec{j}^{Q}(\vec{r}, t) &=\int[d k](\varepsilon-\mu) \dot{\vec{r}} \delta f \\ &=-\int[d k] \tau(\varepsilon-\mu) \vec{v} \vec{v} \cdot\left[e \vec{G}+\frac{(\varepsilon-\mu)}{T} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \vec{r}}\right]\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \\ &= L _{21} \vec{G}+ L _{22}\left(-\frac{\nabla T}{T}\right) \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(L _{22}\) is \(\kappa T\)
  • \(\kappa\) is thermal conductivity tensor

Coefficients of transport (matrices)

\[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \vec{j} \\ \vec{j}^{Q} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{cc} L _{11} & L _{12} \\ L _{21} & L _{22} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} \vec{G} \\ -\nabla T / T \end{array}\right) \]

where \[ \begin{array}{ll} L _{11}=e^{2} \int[d k] \tau \vec{v} \vec{v}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) & L _{12}=-e \int[d k] \tau\left(\varepsilon_{k}-\mu\right) \vec{v} \vec{v}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \\ L _{21}=-e \int[d k] \tau\left(\varepsilon_{k}-\mu\right) \vec{v} \vec{v}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) & L _{22}=\int[d k] \tau\left(\varepsilon_{k}-\mu\right)^{2} \vec{v} \vec{v}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \end{array} \] One example of Onsager relation

They are of the form \[ \Lambda_{i j}^{(v)}=e^{2} \int[d k] \tau\left(\varepsilon_{k}-\mu\right)^{v} v_{i} v_{j}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \] where

  • \(L _{11}= \Lambda ^{(0)}\)
  • \(L _{12}= L _{21}=\frac{1}{(-e)} \Lambda ^{(1)}\)
  • \(L _{22}=\frac{1}{(-e)^{2}} \Lambda ^{(2)}\)

Define energy-resolved conductivity

\[ \sigma_{i j}(\varepsilon)=e^{2} \int[d k] \tau_{\varepsilon} v_{i} v_{j} \delta\left(\varepsilon_{k}-\varepsilon\right) \] then \[ \Lambda_{i j}^{(v)}=\int d \varepsilon(\varepsilon-\mu)^{v}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \sigma_{i j}(\varepsilon) \] For \(T =0\)

  • \(\Lambda ^{(0)}= \sigma \left(\varepsilon_{F}\right)\)
  • \(\Lambda ^{(1)}=\frac{\pi^{2}}{3}\left(k_{B} T\right)^{2} \sigma ^{\prime}\left(\varepsilon_{F}\right)\)
  • \(\Lambda ^{(2)}=\frac{\pi^{2}}{3}\left(k_{B} T\right)^{2} \sigma \left(\varepsilon_{F}\right)\)

Conductivity Law

这部分只考虑电场，没有温度差

consider \(\nabla T =0\)

  1. Ohm's law:
    \[ \vec{j}=\sigma \vec{G} \]

  2. Fick's law for "diffusion current": \[ \vec{j}=- D \nabla \rho \]

其中 \(\vec{G} \equiv \vec{E}+\frac{1}{e} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}\) ，所以需要求出 \(\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}\)

For electron gas at low \(T\) \[ \mu \cong \varepsilon_{F}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m^{*}}\left(3 \pi^{2} n\right)^{2 / 3} \] where \(\rho=-e n\) then \[ \frac{\partial \varepsilon_{F}}{\partial \vec{r}}=\frac{2}{3} \frac{\varepsilon_{F}}{n} \frac{\partial n}{\partial \vec{r}}=\frac{2}{3} \frac{\varepsilon_{F}}{\rho} \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial \vec{r}} \] therefore \[ \begin{aligned} \vec{j}&=\sigma \vec{G} \\ &=\sigma (\vec{E}+\frac{1}{e} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}) \\ &= \frac{\sigma}{e} \frac{\partial \mu}{\partial \vec{r}}\\ &\cong \frac{\sigma}{e} \frac{\partial \varepsilon_{F}}{\partial \vec{r}}\\ &= \frac{\sigma}{e} \frac{2}{3} \frac{\varepsilon_{F}}{\rho} \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial \vec{r}}\\ &=-\left[\frac{\sigma}{(-e)} \frac{2}{3} \frac{\varepsilon_{F}}{\rho} \right] \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial \vec{r}}\\ &=- D \nabla \rho \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(D =\frac{1}{(-e)} \frac{2 \varepsilon_{F}}{3 \rho} \sigma =\frac{1}{e^{2} g\left(\varepsilon_{F}\right)} \sigma\) is Einstein relation (for degenerate electron gas)
  • \(\nabla \rho=\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial \vec{r}}\)

example for T=0

At \(T=0\)，费米狄拉克分布是阶梯函数 \(\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right)=\delta\left(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{F}\right)\) \[ \begin{aligned} \sigma_{i j} &=e^{2} \int[d k] \tau_{\varepsilon} v_{i} v_{j} \delta\left(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{F}\right) \\ &=e^{2} \tau_{\varepsilon_{F}} g\left(\varepsilon_{F}\right)\left\langle v_{i} v_{j}\right\rangle_{F S} \end{aligned} \] where \(\left\langle v_{i} v_{j}\right\rangle_{F S}\) is an integral over the \(FS\) \[ D_{i j}=\tau_{\varepsilon_{F}}\left\langle v_{i} v_{j}\right\rangle_{F S} \] For isotropic diffusion \[ D_{x x}=\tau_{\varepsilon_{F}} \frac{1}{3}\left\langle v^{2}\right\rangle_{F S}=\frac{1}{3} v_{F}^{2} \tau_{\varepsilon_{F}} \]

Alternative form of conductivity

\[ \sigma_{i j}=e^{2} \int[d k] \tau v_{i} v_{j}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \]

where \(v_{i} v_{j}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right)=v_{i} \frac{\partial \varepsilon}{\hbar \partial k_{j}}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right)=v_{i}\left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\hbar \partial k_{j}}\right)\) then \[ \begin{aligned} \sigma_{i j} &=e^{2} \tau \int[d k]\left[-\frac{\partial\left(f_{0} v_{i}\right)}{\hbar \partial k_{j}}+f_{0} \frac{\partial v_{i}}{\hbar \partial k_{j}}\right] \\ &=e^{2} \tau \int[d k] f_{0} \cdot m_{i j}^{*-1} \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(\tau\) is a const.

Hall effect

classical approach

equation of motion \[ m^{*} \frac{d \vec{v}}{d t}=-e \vec{E}-e \frac{\vec{v}}{c} \times \vec{B}-m^{*} \frac{\vec{v}}{\tau} \] $ d / d t=$ at steady state, m field \(\vec{B}=B \hat{z} ;\) \[ \left(\begin{array}{cc} m^{*} / \tau & e B / c \\ -e B / c & m^{*} / \tau \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} v_{x} \\ v_{y} \end{array}\right)=-e\left(\begin{array}{c} E_{x} \\ E_{y} \end{array}\right) \] since \(\vec{j}=-e n \vec{v}\), we have \[ \begin{aligned} \left(\begin{array}{c} E_{x} \\ E_{y} \end{array}\right)&=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \frac{m^{*}}{n e^{2} \tau} & \frac{B}{n e c} \\ -\frac{B}{n e c} & \frac{m^{*}}{n e^{2} \tau} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} j_{x} \\ j_{y} \end{array}\right) \\ &=\rho_{0}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & \omega_{c} \tau \\ -\omega_{c} \tau & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} j_{x} \\ j_{y} \end{array}\right) \end{aligned} \] where

  • \(\rho_{0}=\frac{m^{*}}{n e^{2} \tau}\)
  • \(\omega_{c}=\frac{e B}{m^{*} c}\)

conductivity \[ \sigma = \rho ^{-1}=\frac{\sigma_{0}}{1+\left(\omega_{c} \tau\right)^{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\omega_{c} \tau \\ \omega_{c} \tau & 1 \end{array}\right) \] where \(\sigma_{0}=\frac{n e^{2} \tau}{m^{*}}\)

the limitation

  1. for \(\omega_{c} \tau<<1\)

\[ \sigma =\frac{\sigma_{0}}{1+\left(\omega_{c} \tau\right)^{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\omega_{c} \tau \\ \omega_{c} \tau & 1 \end{array}\right) {\longrightarrow}\quad \sigma_{0}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\omega_{c} \tau \\ \omega_{c} \tau & 1 \end{array}\right) \]

  1. for \(\omega_{c} \tau>>1\)

\[ \sigma =\frac{\sigma_{0}}{1+\left(\omega_{c} \tau\right)^{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\omega_{c} \tau \\ \omega_{c} \tau & 1 \end{array}\right) {\longrightarrow}\quad \left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 & -n e c / B \\ n e c / B & 0 \end{array}\right) \]

semiclassical approach

equation of motion \[ \hbar \frac{d \vec{k}}{d t}=-e \vec{E}-\frac{e}{c} \vec{v}_{k} \times \vec{B} \] Recall "Chamber's formulation" (without density and T gradients) \[ \begin{aligned} \delta f &=\int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} e^{-(t-t) / \tau} \vec{v}_{k} \cdot[-\hbar \dot{\vec{k}}] \frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon} \\ &=-e \int_{-\infty}^{t} d t^{\prime} e^{-(t-t) / \tau} \vec{v}_{k} \cdot\left(\vec{E}+\frac{1}{c} \vec{v}_{k} \times \vec{B}\right) \left(-\frac{\partial f_{0}}{\partial \varepsilon}\right) \end{aligned} \] We can now only count on \(v_{k}\) for magnetic effect
