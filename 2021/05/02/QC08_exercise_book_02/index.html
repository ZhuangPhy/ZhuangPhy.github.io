Chapter 1 Qubits

Problem 1.

We denote two orthonormal states of a single qubit as \[ \{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\} \] where \[ \langle 0 \mid 0\rangle=\langle 1 \mid 1\rangle=1, \quad\langle 0 \mid 1\rangle=\langle 1 \mid 0\rangle=0 . \] Any state of this system can be written as a superposition (linear combination of the states) \[ \alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle, \quad|\alpha|^{2}+|\beta|^{2}=1, \quad \alpha, \beta \in C \] Find a parameter representation (i) if the underlying field is the set of real numbers (ii) if the underlying field is the set of complex numbers.

Problem 2.

Consider the normalized states \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{1} \\ \sin \theta_{1} \end{array}\right), \quad\left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{2} \\ \sin \theta_{2} \end{array}\right) . \] Find the condition on \(\theta_{1}\) and \(\theta_{2}\) such that \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{1} \\ \sin \theta_{1} \end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{2} \\ \sin \theta_{2} \end{array}\right) \] is normalized.

Problem 3.

Let \(\{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\}\) be an orthonormal basis in the Hilbert space \(R ^{2}\). Let \[ A:=|0\rangle\langle 0|+| 1\rangle\langle 1| \] Consider the three cases \[ \begin{array}{ll} \text { (i) }|0\rangle:=\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right), & |1\rangle:=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \\ \text { (ii) }|0\rangle:=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right), & |1\rangle:=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) \\ \text { (iii) }|0\rangle:=\left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta \\ \sin \theta \end{array}\right), & |1\rangle:=\left(\begin{array}{c} \sin \theta \\ -\cos \theta \end{array}\right) . \end{array} \]

Problem 4.

Let \(\{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\}\) be an orthonormal basis in the Hilbert space \(C ^{2}\). The NOT operation (unitary operator) is defined as \[ |0\rangle \rightarrow|1\rangle, \quad|1\rangle \rightarrow|0\rangle . \] (i) Find the unitary operator \(U_{N O T}\) which implements the NOT operation with respect to the basis \(\{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\}\). (ii) Let \[ |0\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right), \quad|1\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) . \] Find the matrix representation of \(U_{N O T}\) for this basis. (iii) Let \[ |0\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right), \quad|1\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) \] Find the matrix representation of \(U_{N O T}\) for this basis.

Problem 1.

We denote two orthonormal states of a single qubit as \[ \{|0\rangle,|1\rangle\} \] where \[ \langle 0 \mid 0\rangle=\langle 1 \mid 1\rangle=1, \quad\langle 0 \mid 1\rangle=\langle 1 \mid 0\rangle=0 . \] Any state of this system can be written as a superposition (linear combination of the states) \[ \alpha|0\rangle+\beta|1\rangle, \quad|\alpha|^{2}+|\beta|^{2}=1, \quad \alpha, \beta \in C \] Find a parameter representation (i) if the underlying field is the set of real numbers (ii) if the underlying field is the set of complex numbers.

Solution 1. (i) Using \(\cos \theta, \sin \theta\) and the identity \[ \cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta=1 \] for all \(\theta \in R\) we have \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta \\ \sin \theta \end{array}\right) . \] (ii) We have as a representation \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} e^{i \phi} \cos \theta \\ \sin \theta \end{array}\right) \] where \(\phi \in R\) and \(e^{i \phi} e^{-i \phi}=1\).

Problem 2.

Consider the normalized states \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{1} \\ \sin \theta_{1} \end{array}\right), \quad\left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{2} \\ \sin \theta_{2} \end{array}\right) . \] Find the condition on \(\theta_{1}\) and \(\theta_{2}\) such that \[ \left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{1} \\ \sin \theta_{1} \end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{c} \cos \theta_{2} \\ \sin \theta_{2} \end{array}\right) \] is normalized.

Solution 2. From the condition that the vector \[ \left(\begin{array}{l} \cos \theta_{1}+\cos \theta_{2} \\ \sin \theta_{1}+\sin \theta_{2} \end{array}\right) \] is normalized it follows that \[ \left(\sin \theta_{1}+\sin \theta_{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\cos \theta_{1}+\cos \theta_{2}\right)^{2}=1 \] Thus we have \[ \sin \theta_{1} \sin \theta_{2}+\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}=-\frac{1}{2} \] It follows that \[ \cos \left(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}\right)=-\frac{1}{2} \] Therefore, \(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}=2 \pi / 3\) or \(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}=4 \pi / 3\)
